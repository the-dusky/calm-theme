Keep Calm and Go Outside

Calm Outdoors, Thailand's first camping fashion brand, makes clothing that meets the needs of city people who are lost in the forest.

Calm Outdoors, a Thai camping fashion brand that sells throughout Asia, with items that meet the needs of city people who want to go camping in the woods.
Story by Thanyarat Kotwantha, picture by Maninuch Boonruang
Home / Business / The Entrepreneur
July 9, 2024
Share on
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
5K

The rain poured down, turning the mountains into a cool green, a shade that is hard to find in other seasons. Some people use their cameras to capture the moment, while others use their eyes to absorb the sweet memories. Ton -Nattapong Busabong, founder of Calm Outdoors, chose to capture the moment on the colors of a stylish hat.

“When you are on the top of a mountain during the rainy season, you will see this green color. It is a familiar image that makes you feel that this is the color of the rainy season forest. So we named it Rain Forest. It is a design work in the style of Calm Outdoors.” 

Calm Outdoors is Thailand’s first camping fashion brand. Their products range from waterproof and UV-resistant hats to quick-drying, comfortable swim trunks, meeting both design and outdoor use, as well as environmentally-conscious manufacturing processes.

Although it was launched in 2018, around the same time as COVID-19, the company was able to navigate the crisis and eventually expanded to overseas markets such as Hong Kong, Singapore, and Taiwan, with overwhelming response. It has also partnered with many famous artists and brands, one of which is The Cloud, who will soon have a fun project together.

If you are one of those people who are always on the go, or someone who is 'in the city but in the forest', we believe that once you get to know Calm Outdoors better, you will fall in love with this brand just like we do.

A camping brand that combines both function and fashion.

“My father runs a shoe factory, so I started buying raw materials since I was 15,” Ton recalls his involvement in the fashion industry since he was a child. When he grew up, his first job was selling raw materials for making clothing, and then expanding into a jeans clothing brand. However, the difficulty of selling jeans is that the customer base is quite limited.

“Let’s say 10 people walk in, 5 men and 5 women. And out of those 5 men, only 4 wear jeans. Of those 4, only 2 might buy from us. The same goes for women. So we wanted to have a brand that could meet the needs of those 10 people. So we turned to making a brand that is more unisex and can be worn by the whole family.”

At that time, the trend of eco-friendly products and camping products was growing. Ton himself was a big camper, so he saw some opportunities in our market. That is, most camping products in Thailand tend to focus more on functionality than beauty, and are more suitable for wearing in the forest than for everyday use. Ton therefore had the idea to create a brand that people could wear while camping or in everyday life, because it had both 'function' and 'fashion' at the same time. Most importantly, it had to care about nature and be friendly to the environment. 

“If we are an outdoor camping fashion brand, we should also protect the environment so that nature can stay with us for a long time,” Ton explained his intention.

“We want to be in every activity people do. For example, if you ride a bike, you might have a Calm Outdoors bag. If you run, you might have our socks. If you go hiking, you might have our hat, sun protection or rain cover. Or if you go to the mall, you might wear our T-shirt. Because we want to be present in every context of life.” 

The first product of Calm Outdoors is a 'hat' that is waterproof, UV-resistant, lightweight, easy to carry, and looks cool when worn. Although it is an item with a simple appearance and function, it is easily accessible to all ages and genders. It has become a flagship item that is always sold out. 

When the hats started to become popular, Ton expanded to other products such as shirts, pants, bags, and even camping equipment. They also come in many colors and styles. Previously, they were usually eye-friendly and blended in with nature. However, some collections are now starting to have brighter colors.

“These colors, if they stay for a long time, they will become dull and boring. Also, some people may find these colors lonely and gloomy. We think there should be some cheerful colors. Some collections must have colors that are fun to look at because the Calm Outdoors camp is for teenagers. Also, today we are not just camping clothes, but fashionable clothes that can be worn to camp.

“Like this pair of pants, at that time the slogan was ‘The body is in the city, but the heart is in the forest’. They were hiking pants that had to have this and that function. We changed the fabric, changed the number of zippered pockets to be less, until it became a style that can be worn in everyday life or to work, while still maintaining the camping and Calm Outdoors vibe.”

Recently, Ton whispered that he is producing a lightweight raincoat that can be folded and put in a trouser pocket to solve the problem of people who are too lazy to carry an umbrella but do not want to carry a thick raincoat. He plans to release it in late rainy season and early winter.

The body is in the city, the heart is in the forest.

The name Calm Outdoors conveys the calmness of the outdoors, amidst nature and forests. Ton chose this word because, besides Calm meaning 'calm', it is also pronounced similar to the Thai word 'indigo', which is a calm color that has also become the color of the brand's logo. As for the elephant mascot, besides representing Thailand, elephants are also deep and calm animals, giving off a sense of calm, as the brand name suggests. The same goes for products related to nature, with colors and patterns based on sand and trees and grass, as well as production that harms nature as little as possible.  

“We choose materials using recycled fabric first. If there is no recycled fabric, we will use new fabric,” Ton explained about the production process that focuses on using recycled fabric as the main ingredient. The cuteness of this brand is that it tells consumers which pieces are new and which are recycled.

“For any product that is New Material, I will have a sign apologizing to the customer, apologizing to the world that I have to use new fabric. For any product that is recycled fabric, the elephant mascot will sit and smile. The fabric strip is green. There is a message saying, I love the world. I try not to leave any waste. And there are some models that are Zero Waste. We will ask the factory what fabric they have left and then collect those fabrics to produce again.”

In addition to the materials inside, the exterior design also reflects the intention to be a fashion brand that is responsible for the environment. 

“Like the shirt design, it says, ‘A tree that takes 3 minutes to cut down will take 30 years to grow.’ We tried to use the work as a parody to communicate something,” Ton said, turning his back to the picture of a tree stump that had been cut down, along with the message on his shirt. 

Join hands with Thai brands to grow together

Although it sounds like a good concept and has a chance in the market, the journey will not be easy. Since its first launch, the brand that invites people to get out of the house, Calm Outdoors, has been hit by lockdown measures during the pandemic that have hindered its rise.

“In 2018, we were still able to sell some products, and we attended a few events. Luckily, we were able to sell them in multi-brand stores in two shopping malls. When the lockdown came, we were still able to sell some products online. When the shopping malls opened, people went back to shopping malls. But when the second and third waves came, things got really bad. So we had to freeze ourselves for over a year.”

After gritting their teeth through COVID-19, people started spending money and the camping trend was on the rise, so Calm Outdoors' products were back on sale. 

On the day he stood firm, Ton didn't stop there. He expanded his customer base by joining hands with artists and brands to do fun projects without any limits.

“The first person we worked with was Ray MacDonald. At that time, he started making motorcycle touring videos. We made a hat for Ray. The function was to protect from the sun, UV rays, and easy to carry in a bag. When we started, he wanted to have a product, while we wanted models. So we said, ‘You can be my model.’ It was a win-win situation for both of us.”

When thinking of a collaboration, some people might think of a mix of logos and colors of two brands that people still remember. However, Ton doesn't put any boundaries on this and even tells people to go outside the box as much as they want.

“When it comes to collaborations, I cut out CI Branding because we don’t want to set boundaries or block creative ideas. And giving freedom over the logo becomes another kind of challenge.

“We want to have a good experience working with everyone and every brand. Go ahead and do it. In the end, your identity and Calm Outdoors are clear. So, go for it. One reason we want to work with many brands, especially Thai brands, is so that we can stick together and grow together. So, let’s release everything.”

With such an open and friendly atmosphere, it is no surprise that Calm Outdoors has neighbors visiting to do fun projects both in Thailand and abroad. The Cloud has become one of Calm Outdoors' neighbors, joining in on a special hat collection project. Ton also whispered that by the end of this year, there will be collaborations with 3 international brands.

Calm Move without stopping

Over the past 5-6 years, Calm Outdoors has grown to meet its intended goals, both in terms of making products that are intentional and expanding internationally.

“The first country was Hong Kong. They came to visit Thailand and saw our products at Siam Paragon. When they bought them, they felt that they were comfortable to wear and the price was good, so they contacted them to buy them to sell.

“But now our main markets in Taiwan and Singapore are growing a lot. Let’s say they are growing beyond our expectations. For example, if we calculate that we will sell 100, in one month, we might have already crossed 300. We think that price is the key factor.

“Today, we are supported. To be honest, 50% of Calm Outdoors’ customers are from overseas. So we can circulate this business faster. We also have to develop better and more comprehensive products. Because if we buy foreign brands to compare with us, they will be far ahead. We have to develop ourselves to follow. Any business that has high competition, competition that does not cut prices, we will want to push ourselves to be better. Because if you cut prices, you will be limited by the cost. I can’t use this because I have this much cost. But if we compete in the matter of I have to be better. Everyone will dare to put in what we think is the best. And when the product is strong, the brand will be strong as well.”

Although Calm Outdoors has grown beyond expectations overseas, Ton told us that he would like to see a higher proportion of Thai customers than at present. A major challenge in the Thai fashion market is the fierce competition between Thai brands, foreign brands, and second-hand clothing from abroad. Pricing and expanding customer bases are still tough challenges for the brand, because Calm Outdoors is in the middle between brands that focus on functionality and affordable prices, and big brands that sell at sky-high prices.

“The difficulty is how to make the first group of people understand why we are selling more expensively, and how to make the upper and middle groups accept us. So we see it as a big issue, but it’s not yet time. We think it might not be this year. Our brand needs to be stronger than this. 

“We want Thai brands to be stronger. We want to build trust in our products so that Thai people dare to try our products and other brands. If the old image, we admit that it is quite difficult to make each product good because of volume. But today, we have more options. We believe that Thai brands are not inferior to foreign brands. If there is support, it will circulate and make the brand stronger. When the brand is stronger, people will dare to use better products and gradually upgrade it.”

Before parting, we looked at the soothing green hat that Ton had mentioned with a different feeling. Because deep down inside the external beauty was the story of the strong determination of this Thai brand to produce good quality products to make life more convenient for people living 'outside the house', along with taking care of the 'outside the house' that we visited, especially the forest and peaceful nature, so that it will remain with us for a long time.

Lessons Learned

  • Starting with what you are interested in and know for sure helps you to pinpoint the pain points in the market and make it easier to make a difference.
  • Branding is not just about logos or CI, but the identity and intention of the brand that people remember.
  • Constantly looking for areas for further development will keep your business from stagnating.
